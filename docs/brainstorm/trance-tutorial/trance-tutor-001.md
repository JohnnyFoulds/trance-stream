To the uninitiated listener, the sweeping soundscapes and driving energy of trance music can sound incredibly complex. For example, a track written in C minor might start with the root note, jump up to the third, and then dramatically drop down to the sixth degree in a lower octave before walking back up to the seventh to create a sense of resolution. The unique emotional resonance of trance music is born from how it utilizes this music theory. The minor scale is traditionally associated with sadness or melancholy in Western music theory.
